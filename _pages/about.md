I am a **PhD Candidate in Information Science at Cornell University**, advised by [Cristobal Cheyre](https://infosci.cornell.edu/faculty/cristobal-cheyre), Qian Yang, and Malte Ziewitz.

My research focuses on emerging technologies, particularly **Artificial Intelligence (AI)**, and how they can be governed and harnessed to address social and environmental challenges while preventing harm. I integrate insights from technology policy, Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), and participatory design to enhance policymaking as a more participatory and adaptive process shaped by technological innovation.

My work advances two interconnected strands:
1. **Pragmatic Governance:** Examining flexible governance systems (such as regulatory sandboxes) that enable governments to design adaptive policy instruments in response to uncertainty.
2. **Participatory AI Infrastructures:** Examining how AI-mediated could enhance policymaking, making it more inclusive, deliberative, and scalable.

### Professional Experience
Beyond academia, I am the co-founder of **[Foresight Consulting](http://www.foresight.cl/)**. I have served as an advisor to the Ministry of Economy and the Ministry of Science in Chile, and I currently work with international organizations such as **UNESCO** and the **IDB** on AI readiness assessments and governance strategies in Latin America.

I hold an MSc in Information Science from Cornell University and an MSc in Industrial and Systems Engineering from Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile.
