I am a **PhD Candidate in Information Science at Cornell University**, advised by [Cristobal Cheyre](https://infosci.cornell.edu/faculty/cristobal-cheyre), Qian Yang, and Malte Ziewitz.

My research focuses on the governance of emerging technologies, particularly **Artificial Intelligence (AI)**. I study how these technologies can be harnessed to address social and environmental challenges while preventing harm. By integrating insights from technology policy, **Human-Computer Interaction (HCI)**, and participatory design, I aim to make policymaking a more adaptive process that is responsive to technological innovation.

My work advances two interconnected research strands:

1.  **Pragmatic Governance:** I examine how governments design flexible governance systems—such as regulatory sandboxes—to create adaptive policy instruments in response to uncertainty.
2.  **Participatory AI Infrastructures:** I develop and study AI-mediated tools (using NLP and Generative AI) that can enhance civic participation, making it more inclusive, deliberative, and scalable.

### Professional Experience

Beyond academia, I work at the intersection of policy and practice. I am the co-founder of **[Foresight Consulting](http://www.foresight.cl/)**, where we help organizations navigate complex future challenges.

Previously, I served as an advisor to the **Ministry of Economy** and the **Ministry of Science** in Chile, focusing on the future of work and social adoption of technology. I currently collaborate with international organizations, including **UNESCO** and the **Inter-American Development Bank (IDB)**, leading AI readiness assessments and governance strategies for governments across Latin America.
